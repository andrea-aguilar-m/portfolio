Proserauto

Revenue Growth During the Pandemic

Proserauto S.A. · Automotive · Ecuador · 2020–2022

Diversified a company's revenue streams during COVID-19, grew revenue by 150% in 6 months, and achieved 1,300% ROI on paid campaigns as the sole marketer with a lean budget.

Growth Marketing Performance Marketing Email Marketing

The Challenge

Proserauto was a high-performance equipment company for 4x4 vehicles with a single location, two sales representatives, and an average monthly revenue of ~$100,000. When the COVID-19 pandemic hit in 2020, demand for high-performance equipment dropped sharply and the company had no recurring revenue stream, low digital presence, and no CRM to fall back on. As the sole marketer, I was responsible for finding a way to keep the business alive and growing during mandatory curfews and a collapsed primary market.

My Role

Marketing Manager; sole marketer with full ownership of strategy, execution, budget, and a direct line to the owner.

Approach

  • Conducted benchmarking and supplier analysis to identify viable recurring services, landing on oil changes and vehicle maintenance as the highest-potential opportunity given the existing client base and infrastructure.
  • Developed special packages and pricing for the new recurring services, balancing competitiveness with margin viability during a period of reduced overall demand.
  • Launched the first paid advertising campaigns for the company across Instagram and Facebook, promoting the new recurring services, growing recurring service customers from 0 to 300 in the first month, despite mandatory curfews.
  • Developed email marketing campaigns targeting existing high-revenue clients and new prospects for both recurring services and high-performance products, achieving an average 30% open rate.
  • Conducted extensive ad optimization: testing copy, visuals, posting timing, and audience targeting to identify the most profitable segments, achieving 1,300% ROI on advertising investment of approximately 1% of revenue.
  • Implemented HubSpot CRM to track opportunities, identify recurring and loyal customers, and introduced a loyalty program for clients spending over $20,000/month, including special discounts and invitations to sponsored events like races and off-road excursions post-pandemic.
  • Built a product wagon framework for the sales team to identify and capture cross-selling and upselling opportunities across the product range.
  • Built the company website on Squarespace: mapped content architecture, wrote all product descriptions, selected imagery, and optimized user flow and experience.

Key Learnings

  • Constraints force creativity. A mandatory curfew, a collapsed primary market, and a budget of 1% of revenue left no room for waste; every decision had to be precise, and that precision is what drove the results.
  • Diversification is a marketing strategy. Identifying, pricing, and launching a new service line wasn't just a business decision; it required marketing thinking from day one: benchmarking, positioning, packaging, and promotion.
  • Loyalty compounds. The clients who were nurtured through the pandemic with special packages and personalized outreach became the most valuable long-term customers. Retention built during a crisis outlasts the crisis itself.
  • Owning everything teaches you fast. Being the sole marketer owning strategy, execution, budget, reporting, and sales alignment meant there was no one else to blame and no room to specialize. It built a kind of marketing instinct that broader teams rarely develop.
