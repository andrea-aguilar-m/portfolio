Danone Waters

Global Advocacy Strategy & Content

Danone · FMCG · Paris, France · 2025

Led three global advocacy projects for Danone's Waters category, coordinating teams across four countries and two external agencies to deliver a playbook, an e-learning course, and an internal campaign; all from Paris.

Content Strategy Stakeholder Management Global Marketing

The Challenge

Danone's Waters category operates across markets worldwide, but country teams needed the tools, confidence, and guidance to advocate effectively for the category at a local level. Internally, Danone employees needed a structured way to understand and communicate the Waters category's value and sustainability initiatives. At the same time, the category needed timely internal campaigns to activate key moments in the calendar year. My mission as Advocacy & Sustainability Intern was to deliver three interconnected projects that would address all of these gaps, simultaneously, within a 6-month internship, coordinating multiple internal stakeholders across four countries and external agencies.

My Role

As Global Waters Category Advocacy & Sustainability Intern, I owned three parallel workstreams end-to-end, reporting directly to the Senior Director.

Approach

Workstream 1: Advocacy Playbook for Country Business Units
  • Gathered input from Public Affairs, Internal and External Communications teams to define the structure and content of the playbook.
  • Reached out to Country Business Units (CBUs) in Uruguay, Mexico, and Singapore to collect success case studies to include as practical examples.
  • Briefed and managed an external agency on design and copywriting, providing ongoing feedback on visual presentation and narrative flow.
  • Consolidated a ready-to-use guide for CBUs to implement their own local water advocacy initiatives aligned with the global category strategy.
Workstream 2: E-learning Course for Danone Employees
  • Translated complex sustainability and waters category concepts into accessible, engaging learning content, including flip cards, videos, and interactive modules.
  • Coordinated with Public Affairs, R&I, Water Resilience, External Communications, and Learning teams to gather, verify, and synthesize technical information.
  • Managed two external agencies simultaneously; one for content development and one for UI, synthesizing feedback from multiple stakeholders and communicating clear revisions.
  • Delivered a course advanced to final testing phase, with content, interactive modules, and stakeholder feedback fully incorporated.
Workstream 3: Dry January Internal Advocacy Campaign
  • Defined the campaign brief with clear goals, deliverables, and timelines for the agency.
  • Led a concept development process, reviewing multiple creative directions with the agency until landing on clear, approved paths.
  • Presented the approved concepts to a cross-functional committee including Internal Communications, External Communications, and other stakeholders for final sign-off.
  • Co-developed all campaign assets: key messaging, digital assets, and print materials, leaving the full campaign packaged and ready for execution in early January 2026.

Results

  • 📖 Advocacy Playbook signed off. Fully developed and approved by the Senior Director and internal stakeholders, with success case studies from Country Business Units in Uruguay, Mexico, and Singapore; ready for distribution to global markets.
  • 🎓 E-learning course advanced to final testing phase. Content, structure, and interactive modules completed and validated by stakeholders across Public Affairs, R&I, Water Resilience, and Learning teams.
  • 📅 Dry January campaign fully planned and packaged. All assets, messaging, and print materials ready for execution in early January 2026.
  • 🌍 Teams across 4 countries engaged. France, Uruguay, Mexico, and Singapore actively contributed input and success cases to shape a globally relevant advocacy strategy.

Key Learnings

  • Global relevance starts with local listening. Building an advocacy playbook that would actually work across markets meant understanding local contexts, priorities, and constraints before defining the global framework; not after.
  • Managing agencies is a skill in itself. Clear briefs, timely feedback, and well-defined priorities are what separate agencies that deliver from agencies that drift, regardless of their talent level.
  • Complexity scales with stakeholders, not tasks. Each of the three workstreams was manageable on its own. The challenge was synthesizing feedback from 10+ stakeholders across functions and countries without losing momentum or diluting the output.
  • Internships are what you make them. Owning three global projects and presenting to senior directors within 6 months isn't typical for an internship; it came from treating every deliverable as if it had my name on it permanently.
