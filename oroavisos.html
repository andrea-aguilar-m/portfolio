OroAvisos

Product Launch & GTM Execution

Banco de Machala · Financial Services · 2024

Took ownership of a regulated bank product launch mid-flight and drove it to full deployment across specific channels, becoming the primary internal reference for the product post-launch.

Product Marketing GTM Strategy Product Launch

The Challenge

When I joined Banco de Machala as Product and Innovation Manager, the deployment of OroAvisos (a premium SMS notification service that keeps clients informed about the activity and status of their banking products) was already underway in Q1 2024, but needed a clear owner to drive it to the finish line. The product operated in a highly regulated environment, required precise pricing communication, and needed to be integrated across specific bank channels simultaneously. There was no room for misalignment; commercial teams, customer support, IT, and communications all needed to be moving in the same direction.

My Role

As Product and Innovation Manager, I took over end-to-end GTM and launch execution, owning messaging, channel integration, testing, commercial enablement, and post-launch monitoring.

Approach

  • Collaborated with the communications team to define messaging, positioning, and customer touchpoints, identifying where the product could be promoted most effectively along the customer journey.
  • Developed key messages for commercial and customer support teams, ensuring they could explain the product clearly to clients, including pricing, which needed to be both regulated and competitive in the market.
  • Led final testing before launch in specific channels, ensuring all features worked correctly and coordinating with developers to resolve issues before go-live.
  • Ensured full channel integration across in-person, website, and call center, coordinating across IT, development, and operations to deliver a seamless experience.
  • Monitored post-launch performance and transaction activity during the initial weeks to detect bugs, flag issues, and coordinate improvements in real time.
  • Became the primary internal reference point for OroAvisos; the go-to person for reporting malfunctions, providing detailed product information, and coordinating updates across teams.

Results

  • 🚀 Product successfully launched across bank channels: in-person, website, and call center.
  • 🔧 Post-launch issues identified and resolved within 2 weeks, working closely with Development and QA to diagnose, track, and fix problems in real time.
  • 💬 Commercial and support teams fully enabled with clear messaging and pricing frameworks.
  • 🔗 Became the primary internal reference for product updates, issue resolution, and stakeholder communication post-launch.

Key Learnings

  • Taking over mid-flight is a leadership test. Inheriting a project in motion requires rapid context-building, immediate trust with cross-functional teams, and the ability to move fast without breaking what's already working.
  • Pricing communication is a product decision. In a regulated environment, how you communicate price is as strategic as the price itself; clarity and compliance have to coexist.
  • Launch is not the finish line. The most valuable work happened post-launch: monitoring, resolving issues, and becoming the person every team relied on for answers. Ownership doesn't end at go-live.
  • Channel integration is harder than it looks. Getting in-person, website, and call center aligned simultaneously requires relentless cross-functional coordination and a single point of accountability.
