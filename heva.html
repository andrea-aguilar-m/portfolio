HeVA

SaaS Product Marketing & Launch

SICMA Ecuador · B2B SaaS · 2023–2024

Owned the full product marketing function for a B2B SaaS sustainability platform, from positioning and messaging to demo scripting, sales enablement, and the launch of a new sustainability module.

Product Marketing SaaS GTM Strategy

The Challenge

HeVA is a B2B environmental management platform developed by SICMA Ecuador, an early-stage sustainability consultancy. The platform centralises environmental data, automates compliance processes, generates reports, and monitors key metrics such as carbon footprint in real time. Originally offered as a bundled benefit within service contracts, HeVA had no dedicated product marketing, no standalone identity, and no monetisation strategy. Despite being a technically strong product, HeVA had no clear positioning for different buyer profiles, no sales materials, no demo scripts, and no structured launch strategy for new features. As the first marketing hire, I took on the product marketing function alongside the broader B2B marketing role, translating a highly technical product into messaging that would resonate with sustainability managers, environmental directors, and C-suite decision-makers.

My Role

Marketing Team Lead. Took on full product marketing ownership for HeVA alongside the broader marketing function, reporting directly to the CEO.

Approach

  • Defined ICP profiles and buyer personas for HeVA across different industries, identifying the specific pain points, goals, and decision-making triggers for each segment.
  • Translated complex technical features into clear, accessible positioning and messaging tailored to each ICP, making environmental compliance and carbon tracking concepts understandable for non-technical buyers.
  • Created a dedicated HeVA product page with structured messaging, feature breakdowns, and clear value propositions.
  • Trained and briefed the sales team on HeVA's value proposition per ICP, standardising how the product was presented across different client contexts.
  • Developed a complete sales enablement toolkit: demo videos, sales decks, FAQ documents, and objection-handling guides to support the commercial team in closing deals.
  • Developed the GTM strategy for the planned launch of HeVA's sustainability module, including positioning, messaging, and communication plan for both new prospects and existing clients.
  • Proposed and defined a user engagement strategy for the platform, including push notifications, email alerts, and data submission reminders, prioritising active platform usage and compliance with reporting deadlines.
  • Defined HeVA's international positioning for the US market, aligning messaging with ISO and GRI sustainability guidelines to make the product viable for global clients.

Results

  • 📦 Product marketing function built from scratch: positioning, messaging, and sales enablement; none existed before.
  • 🚀 GTM strategy defined for the sustainability module, including messaging framework, launch assets, and internal rollout plan. Ready for execution at the time of departure.
  • 🌎 International positioning defined for the US market, aligned with ISO and GRI guidelines, enabling HeVA to be offered to global clients.
  • 💬 Sales team fully enabled with scripted demos, objection-handling guides, and video assets, reducing reliance on ad-hoc presentations.

Key Learnings

  • Product marketing without a PMM title is still product marketing. The work of translating technical complexity into buyer-relevant messaging is the same regardless of what your job title says, and doing it without a dedicated function forces you to be more resourceful.
  • ICP clarity unlocks everything else. Once we defined who HeVA was really for (and what each segment cared about), positioning, demos, and sales conversations became significantly more effective.
  • Enablement is the bridge between marketing and revenue. Generating interest is only half the job. The leads only converted when the commercial team could confidently demo, handle objections, and communicate value in the client's language.
  • B2B product marketing can't focus solely on acquisition. HeVA's strongest growth opportunity was expansion within SICMA's existing client base; clients already in regulatory services contracts were natural candidates for upsell into carbon footprint and sustainability modules. That required a different message and a different motion than new business.
